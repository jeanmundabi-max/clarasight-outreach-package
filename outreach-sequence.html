Sequence design notes

Angle rotation logic

Trigger Email 1 — Day 0. Series A is a stage-shift event. New money creates new buying attention. The window to become part of Clarasight's scaling story is now, not after they've already chosen a GTM model.
Friction Email 2 — Day 6. The informal shortlist problem. Enterprise travel vendors get selected through peer networks before an RFP exists. Without a proactive channel, Clarasight surfaces only through GBTA referrals. This is the friction the current model cannot solve.
PVP Email 3 — Day 12. Third-order intelligence from CMS Open Payments. 5 mid-size pharma companies with active HCP compliance windows — identified before the compliance team knows they need a new platform. The email IS the deliverable. No pitch.

Why the angles are different

  • Email 1 and Email 2 use different angle types — Trigger and Friction. Same angle twice = same situation, different words. That is not a sequence.
  • Email 3 (PVP) does not belong to the benchmark 5-line structure. It uses PEA: Preview, Engage, Ask. Researcher tone, not copywriter.
  • CTA types are also rotated: Delivery (Email 1), Question (Email 2), Low-friction offer (Email 3). Three different asks across the sequence.
  • LinkedIn breakup note on Day 8 does not restart the email sequence. It is a third channel reinforcement before the PVP fires.

PVP criteria check — Email 3

  • Independently useful: List of 5 pharma firms in compliance window — usable without any call
  • Related to value prop: Clarasight HCP compliance module is the exact solution
  • Public data cited: CMS Open Payments, cms.gov, March 2026 filing
  • Meaningful insight: Raw filing data translated into a buying window — 7 weeks post-deadline
  • Beyond pain ID: Specific firms + decision makers + why now
  • Information asymmetry: Adam would not derive this from TMC reports or GBTA contacts
  • Concrete and specific: 5 named firms, named CFOs, dollar figures, filing date
