Buying Signal Intelligence

The Five Enterprise Buying Windows

Enterprise travel programmes do not purchase analytics tools on a random schedule. They buy in windows triggered by specific events. MDB identifies these windows using public data and reaches the companies in them before they form an informal shortlist.

Window 1

New Head of Travel Hire

A travel manager job posting is live on LinkedIn Jobs. The new hire is asked to stand up reporting and produce a programme overview in the first 90 days. They have no data infrastructure. Highest purchase-intent window in corporate travel analytics.

How MDB finds these: LinkedIn Jobs search, public, timestamped, company-named. 42 active positions in the United States as of May 2026.

Priority Score: 84/100
Window 2

CSRD and Scope 3 Emissions Mandate

A European-listed company or United States company with European Union operations must report Scope 3 Category 6 (business travel emissions) for Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive compliance.

How MDB finds these: Annual reports, Carbon Disclosure Project database, Environmental Social Governance disclosure gaps.

Priority Score: 82/100
Window 3

Post-Acquisition Consolidation

A company closed a merger and acquisition transaction in the last 12 to 36 months and now has two travel programmes to consolidate. The Chief Financial Officer asks for a consolidated travel and expense view in the first quarter post-close.

How MDB finds these: Crunchbase, Securities and Exchange Commission 8-K filings, trade press.

Priority Score: 78/100
Window 4

Supplier Contract Renewal

A major airline or hotel contract is coming up for renewal in the next 90 days. Procurement walks into a multi-million dollar supplier negotiation without a defensible, complete spend picture.

How MDB finds these: LinkedIn activity monitoring, Global Business Travel Association signals, procurement role requirements.

Priority Score: 74/100
Window 5

Pharmaceutical Healthcare Professional Sunshine Act Reporting

A pharma company must report Healthcare Professional travel spend to the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services by March 31 each year. Non-compliance carries civil monetary penalties of up to $1.17M per year.

How MDB finds these: Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services Open Payments public database (cms.gov/OpenPayments).

Priority Score: 82/100

Compounding

How the Infrastructure Gets Better Over Time

Revenue infrastructure is not a campaign. It compounds. Each of the three loops below improves on its own as data accumulates. After 12 months, the system converts faster, cheaper, and more predictably than anything started from scratch.

Loop 1

The Core Acquisition Loop

Enterprise travel programme in a buying window → MDB identifies and reaches → Clarasight enters evaluation → Sales team qualifies and converts → New Business Travel News 100 customer → Customer referred to peer travel managers at comparable enterprises → MDB identifies referral-ready contacts and engineers the ask → Next enterprise enters buying window already having heard of Clarasight. Each cycle is faster and more efficient than the last.

Loop 2

The Credibility and Case Study Loop

Each new customer provides an outcome metric (99% reduction in reporting time, 90% reduction in manual reconciliation, defensible Scope 3 data produced in 48 hours instead of 6 weeks) → Outcome becomes public case study or press mention → Enterprise buyers in the research phase encounter the metric → They arrive at the demo with more confidence → They convert faster and at higher average contract value. The Bain and Company and Big Four wins are the beginning of this loop.

Loop 3

The Test, Measure, Learn, Scale, Document Loop

MDB runs each 90-day outbound cycle as a structured experiment → Which segments produce the highest reply rates, the best conversations, the fastest conversions? → The most effective segment becomes the primary focus → Messaging is refined based on which buyer scenes produce the most responses → The second cycle starts with higher signal quality than the first → Document the winning approach → Scale. The data is owned permanently.
