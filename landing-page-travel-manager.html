The data problem

Your TMC gives you a report.
It is not your T&E data.

Pain 01 — The TMC Blind Spot
"My TMC reporting only shows what goes through the TMC. We have 15 to 20% of spend on cards that never appears anywhere."

Managed travel programmes typically capture 60 to 85% of actual T&E spend. The rest — direct bookings, card spend, expense reimbursements, ground transport — never appears in your TMC report. You present to the CFO with data that is structurally incomplete. You do not know exactly how much is missing, but you know it is missing.

Pain 02 — The Three Sources Problem
"We have data in three places and no single source of truth. Finance has one number. Travel has another. When the CFO asks, we spend a week reconciling before we can answer."

Most enterprise travel programmes maintain parallel data streams: TMC reporting, expense platform exports, corporate card statements. Each uses different supplier names, different date conventions, different cost centres. Reconciling them before every board report is a quarterly sprint that should not need to happen.

The reporting trap

"I spend more time building the report
than using it."

Pain 03 — The Build vs Use Problem
"I spend more time building the report than using it. By the time the numbers are right, the meeting is tomorrow and there is no time to actually do analysis."

The monthly travel report at most enterprise programmes takes 3 to 8 hours to produce. TMC data export, expense platform extract, card feed, Excel spreadsheet, formatting. Every month. The output is a lagging indicator — it shows where spend went, not where it is going. Analysis is what happens if there is time left.

Pain 04 — The Negotiation Gap
"We walked into the hotel renewal without a defensible number. We knew roughly what we spent, but we could not prove it at the property level. They knew it too."

Hotel suppliers negotiate against programme data. They know their numbers with precision. If you cannot produce a verified breakdown of room nights by property, rate tier, and season — they assume you do not have data. And they will be right. On a $50M programme, that gap is worth $750,000 to $2.5M in recoverable hotel savings per renewal cycle.
