Funnel Cascade

Where leads are won, lost, and left on the table

Today Clarasight operates on four stages — each with the same structural gap: strong execution once a buyer is engaged, weak systematic access to buyers before they are. Each column shows the current state, what changes after MDB installs, and what is missing.

Top of Funnel — Attraction
How enterprise travel programmes discover Clarasight for the first time
🔴 Today
Enterprise buyers discover Clarasight through GBTA peer networks, conference presence (Business Travel Show, GBTA Global), and direct referrals from existing customers. Awareness is limited to buyers who already know Clarasight exists — or who ask a peer who does.
⚠ 1–2 qualified conversations per month
🟢 After MDB Installs
MDB identifies enterprise programmes in active buying windows and reaches them before they ask their peer network. Clarasight enters the buyer's evaluation at step 2 — not step 4. Outreach is specific to the buying trigger: regulatory obligation, cost overrun event, M&A programme integration, or headcount consolidation signal.
✓ Target: 5–8 qualified conversations per month
🟡 What is Missing
  • Systematic outbound to companies in documented buying windows
  • Content that reaches buyers during informal research — before a demo request
  • Authority Engine that keeps Clarasight visible to the 95% not yet in a window
  • Signal detection: LinkedIn job postings, CSRD compliance deadlines, M&A announcements
↓ Buyers enter evaluation
🎯
Middle of Funnel — Alignment
How interested buyers research, compare, and qualify themselves
🔴 Today
Demo-gated pricing. No trial. No self-serve research path. Buyers who want to evaluate independently cannot. No nurture content exists for buyers in the pre-demo phase. Non-ready buyers fall off with no re-engagement mechanism.
⚠ Zero lead nurture infrastructure
🟢 After MDB Installs
MDB qualification identifies live evaluation versus awareness-only buyers before a call is booked. Pre-call brief gives the Clarasight team context: buying window type, pain, decision-maker background, competitive alternatives in consideration. 20-week value-only nurture for non-ready contacts keeps Clarasight in front of the buyer until the window opens.
✓ 20-week nurture installed — no buyer is lost
🟡 What is Missing
  • Lead nurture sequence (currently zero — no mechanism to stay in front of non-ready buyers)
  • Comparison pages for buyers independently evaluating Clarasight versus SAP Concur and internal BI
  • Content capturing buyers in the informal research phase before they contact sales
  • Lead scoring to identify when cold contacts enter a live buying window
↓ Qualified buyer enters sales
⚡
Bottom of Funnel — Activation
How evaluation converts to a signed contract
🔴 Today
Adam and the founding team run sales personally. Conversion is high — warm referral baseline around 30% — but volume is entirely constrained by founder bandwidth. The bottleneck is not conversion. It is throughput. Adam cannot book more calls than he can personally handle.
⚠ Ceiling = founder calendar capacity
🟢 After MDB Installs
MDB qualifies before the handoff. The sales team enters each call knowing: buying window type, decision-maker background, specific pain, competitive alternatives being considered. Outbound-sourced buyers convert at a higher rate because they have already seen proof relevant to their specific situation before the first call.
✓ Qualified handoffs only — no cold demos
🟡 What is Missing
  • Sales enablement content differentiated by buying window type (CSRD buyer vs cost-reduction buyer vs M&A integration buyer)
  • Competitive battlecards: SAP Concur versus Clarasight and Internal BI versus Clarasight
  • Referral engineering from existing BTN 100 wins — each win is an untapped referral trigger
  • Defined handoff process from MDB qualification to Clarasight sales team
↓ Contract signed
🔄
Post-Sale — Expansion and Referral
How closed customers compound into the next acquisition cycle
🔴 Today
Customer success and expansion are actively happening — the 10x growth trajectory is evidence. But the referral engine is entirely informal. No systematic process for case study production or for engineering referral introductions from happy customers. Each BTN 100 win is a one-time outcome rather than an active compounding asset.
⚠ Referral engine: informal, not engineered
🟢 After MDB Installs
Referral engineering identifies which customers are referral-ready and engineers the ask at the right moment (renewal, outcome milestone, GBTA event). Each BTN 100 win becomes an active referral trigger. Bain and Company, Big Four anonymised results, and the Scope 3 compliance wins are packaged into case studies that feed the ToFu loop directly.
✓ Each win feeds the next acquisition cycle
🟡 What is Missing
  • Case study production workflow — two Big Four customer outcomes are not yet systematised
  • Systematic referral ask at renewal — timed to outcome milestone, not left to chance
  • Community touchpoints at GBTA events for brand amplification by existing customers
  • Anonymisation templates for regulated customers who cannot be named publicly
Growth Loops

Three self-reinforcing loops that compound over time

Each loop connects the output of one cycle to the input of the next. At 90 days, they run in parallel. At 12 months, the loops are the acquisition engine — not individual campaigns.

01
Core Acquisition Loop
Signal to conversation to case study to next signal
1
Buying window detected
Enterprise travel programme enters an active buying window — regulatory obligation, cost overrun, M&A integration, or headcount restructure.
Signal source: LinkedIn job posts, CSRD filings, M&A announcements, CMS data
2
Buying-window-specific outreach reaches decision-maker
MDB reaches the programme manager or Head of Travel before the informal shortlist forms. Message is specific to the trigger — not a generic Clarasight pitch.
Timing advantage: buyer is at step 2, not step 4 of their journey
3
Clarasight enters evaluation early
Prospect receives a relevant, proof-backed package before they have formed a shortlist. Clarasight is already in consideration when they begin formal evaluation.
4
Qualified handoff to Clarasight sales team
MDB passes the pre-qualified opportunity with buying window context, pain identified, and competitive alternatives noted. Sales team enters the first call informed.
Baseline: warm referral conversion ~30%
5
New BTN 100 enterprise customer
Programme becomes a Clarasight customer. 99% reduction in reporting time. 90% reduction in manual review hours. Defensible Scope 3 emissions data for board reporting.
Outcome metric: 99% reduction in reporting time
↺
Result becomes case study — feeds the next cycle
The customer outcome is packaged as a case study and distributed through MDB outreach to the next cohort of programmes entering buying windows. The loop restarts with stronger proof than the previous cycle.
Loop accelerates: each cycle carries more evidence than the last
Why this compounds: Better signal data, sharper message, and stronger case study proof with each cycle. At cycle 3, MDB knows which company profiles and which buying window types produce the highest quality conversations — and every new campaign starts from that knowledge.
02
Credibility and Case Study Loop
Published outcome data builds trust before the first contact
1
Customer outcome achieved and documented
Clarasight delivers a measurable result for a BTN 100 enterprise customer. 99% reporting time reduction. 90% manual review reduction. Defensible Scope 3 data for board use.
Current status: Bain & Company case study published. Two Big Four outcomes not yet systematised.
2
Anonymised case study or press mention published
Outcome is packaged as an anonymised case study or placed in travel trade press: Business Travel News, BTN Executive, The Company Dime, PhocusWire. The metric reaches buyers outside Clarasight's existing network.
Distribution channels: BTN, trade press, GBTA newsletter, LinkedIn
3
Buyer in informal research phase encounters the outcome
An enterprise travel manager researching their own reporting problem finds the case study during desk research — before they ask a peer, before they book a demo, before Clarasight knows they exist.
4
Buyer arrives at demo with pre-built confidence
Because the buyer has already seen a documented outcome relevant to their situation, the first call begins at a higher trust baseline. The sales team does not spend the first 20 minutes establishing credibility.
Effect: shorter sales cycle, higher conversion on first call
↺
Higher ACV win produces a stronger case study
Bigger outcome with a more recognisable enterprise customer. Each cycle adds a more compelling proof point to the loop. The case study library grows more persuasive with each rotation.
Loop strengthens: more recognised customer names, larger programme sizes, bigger metrics
Current state: This loop is running informally — it is not engineered. The Bain case study exists. Two Big Four outcomes are confirmed but not packaged. MDB installs the production workflow so each new outcome automatically enters the loop rather than staying inside Clarasight's sales deck.
03
Test, Measure, Learn, Scale, Document
Structured 90-day cycles that produce a permanent institutional knowledge asset
1
MDB runs each 90-day cycle as a structured experiment
Every cycle has defined inputs: target segment, buying window type, message variant. Three variables tested per cycle. Results tracked at reply rate, conversation quality, and conversion stage.
Tracked: reply rate by segment, conversation quality, conversion rate by window type
2
Winning segment and message combination documented
At cycle end, MDB identifies which company profile, buying window type, and message variant produced the highest quality conversations. This is written into a Clarasight-specific playbook — not a generic template.
Output: permanent internal document — "Segment X + Window Y + Message Z = highest quality conversations"
3
Second cycle starts from the winning profile first
Next cycle prioritises the highest-performing profile identified in cycle one. Messaging is refined based on buyer feedback from sales calls. The starting quality of cycle two is higher than the starting quality of cycle one.
4
Messaging refined from sales call feedback
Every sales call produces insight: what language the buyer used, what objection came up, which proof point landed. MDB integrates this feedback before the next cycle begins. The channel continuously improves.
Effect: reply rate improves each cycle because the message is being refined on live buyer data
↺
Third cycle reaches full TAM with institutional knowledge
By cycle three, Clarasight possesses a documented understanding of which company types, at which moments, with which messages produce the strongest commercial conversations. That document does not disappear when MDB's engagement ends. It stays on Clarasight's balance sheet permanently.
Permanent asset: the playbook belongs to Clarasight — not rented from an agency
Why this matters: Most agencies hand over a list and disappear. MDB installs the system and documents the institutional knowledge. After 3 cycles, Clarasight's internal team can run this channel themselves. The value compounds after MDB leaves — not just while the engagement is active.
